[h1]The Binding of Isaac: Apostasy[/h1]

The Seven Deadly Sins are done chasing Isaac around the basement. Now you play as them.

Apostasy started as two morons deciding they could make the next Isaac DLC with zero experience in spriting, animation, or coding. We got stuck constantly, figured things out eventually, and now there's a real mod here. Every sin plays completely different, with their own stats, items, and problems.

[h2]What's inside[/h2]
[list]
[*][b]6 new playable characters[/b]: Sloth, Pride, Envy, Gluttony, Lust, and Wrath. Each one plays nothing like the others.
[*][b]Tainted versions of all 6.[/b]
[*][b]Over 70 new items[/b] in themed pools for each sin.
[*][b]15+ new trinkets[/b] plus new pocket items: cards, souls, and some weird numbered tiles.
[*][b]A Lil familiar for every sin.[/b]
[*][b]Every character has their own Birthright effect.[/b]
[*]New HUD stuff, pickups, sounds, and a few secrets you'll have to find yourself.
[/list]

[h2]The Characters[/h2]

[b]Sloth[/b]
Barely moves, barely shoots, hits like a truck. His tears are big, slow, explosive, and do a ton of damage. The downside is he refuses to fight the boss until the rest of the floor is cleared. The boss door stays barred until the work is done. Challenge rooms are always open though, since fighting where you're already standing is less effort than leaving. Also, if you bleed enough in a Sacrifice Room, something might show up.
[i]Birthright: Lazy.[/i]

[b]Pride[/b]
Better stats than everyone else and fully aware of it. Starts with the No! trinket and his signature active item, Reeee!, which fires four lasers out diagonally. The downside is that a big chunk of the item pool just won't show up for him. Mostly stat ups. He doesn't need them anyway. Probably.
[i]Birthright: Gloat. Using Reeee! also triggers the Tower.[/i]

[b]Envy[/b]
Can't shoot. At all. He flies, and every time he gets hit there's a chance he gains an orbital familiar. There are close orbitals, far ones, and zig-zagging ones that upgrade through tiers and do stupid amounts of contact damage. Getting hurt is how he gets stronger, so play accordingly.
[i]Birthright: Wish.[/i]

[b]Gluttony[/b]
Big boy. 4 heart containers, no tears, just a Brimstone beam. He can't walk and shoot at the same time, so you have to stand still, charge, and delete whatever's in front of you. Don't bother with flight items either. He's not getting off the ground.
[i]Birthright: Gobble.[/i]

[b]Lust[/b]
Her tears are weak, but she has a charm aura that turns enemies against each other, and anything that dies while charmed comes back as a permanent friend that follows you between rooms. Let the army do the work.
[i]Birthright: Desire.[/i]

[b]Wrath[/b]
No tears. He throws bombs, and only bombs. Starts with 10, and his explosion damage is absurd. Enemies drop bombs when they die (grab them quick, they disappear), bosses drop a bunch, and if something hits him, he explodes too. Once you hit the Depths he unlocks Deep Pockets and can bank up to 999 bombs. If you run out, you can't attack, so don't run out.
[i]Birthright: Destroy.[/i]

[h2]The Tainted Sins[/h2]

[b]Tainted Sloth[/b]
Almost there, but being lazy about it. In development.

[b]Tainted Pride[/b]
Still deciding exactly how insufferable to make him. In development.

[b]Tainted Envy[/b]
Same deal as Envy, can't shoot, but now your fire keys control a clone that you drive around the room. Orbitals you earn alternate between you and the clone, and every 10 seconds you swap places with it (you get a warning first). Keep track of both bodies or one of them is going to get you killed. Runs on soul hearts only and starts with two black hearts.

[b]Tainted Gluttony[/b]
Still cooking. In development.

[b]Tainted Lust[/b]
Starts with 11 broken hearts and almost no damage, but she's fast and leaves a trail of blood creep everywhere she goes. During boss fights, Empty Hearts spawn around the room. Grab them mid-fight for Devil and Angel deal chance, but they all break if you get hit. Every boss you kill removes one broken heart.

[b]Tainted Wrath[/b]
His health is bomb hearts, and one of them always has a lit fuse. Every 30 seconds it goes off: he takes the damage and a troll bomb drops at his feet. Heart pickups do nothing. He heals by picking up bombs instead, and bosses drop 5 of them. Enemy explosions don't damage him, they just kill him. Get to the boss before the fuses do you in.

[h2]State of the mod[/h2]
Apostasy is a work in progress and everything is subject to change. Most things work. Some things work and we don't know why. If something's broken or feels unbalanced, tell us in the comments and we'll keep it in mind.

Have fun, don't skill-issue.
