[h1]The Binding of Isaac: Apostasy[/h1]

What if the Seven Deadly Sins stopped chasing Isaac around the basement... and picked up the tears themselves?

Apostasy is a DLC-sized content mod born from two morons who thought they could make the next Isaac expansion. Somewhere between not knowing why anything worked and not knowing why anything didn't, an actual mod happened. Now the Sins are playable — each with their own gimmick, stats, items, and terrible life choices.

[h2]What's inside[/h2]
[list]
[*][b]6 new playable characters[/b] — Sloth, Pride, Envy, Gluttony, Lust, and Wrath — each built around a completely different way to play.
[*][b]6 Tainted counterparts[/b] that twist every sin into something meaner.
[*][b]70+ new items[/b] split into themed pools for each sin, from Bowling Bombs to the Regular Cookbook.
[*][b]15+ new trinkets[/b] and a pile of new pocket items — cards, souls (Soul of Pride, Soul of the Boomer...), and mysterious numbered Tiles.
[*][b]A "Lil" familiar for every sin[/b] — collect the whole dysfunctional family.
[*][b]Unique Birthright effects[/b] for every character.
[*]Custom HUD elements, pickups, sounds, and secrets we won't spoil here.
[/list]

[h2]The Characters[/h2]

[b]Sloth[/b]
Slow to move, slow to shoot, hits like a dump truck. Sloth lobs huge explosive tears with a massive damage multiplier — when he can be bothered. The catch: the boss door stays barred until the rest of the floor is cleared. No shortcuts. No skipping work. Challenge Rooms, on the other hand, swing right open — fighting where you stand takes less effort than walking away. They say something is waiting for those who bleed enough in Sacrifice Rooms...
[i]Birthright: Lazy.[/i]

[b]Pride[/b]
Better than you at everything, and he knows it. Above-average stats across the board, the No! trinket, and his signature scream — [b]Reeee![/b] — an active item that blasts four lasers diagonally outward. But Pride is far too good for hand-me-downs: a huge chunk of the item pool (mostly other people's stat-ups) simply refuses to appear for him. He doesn't need the help anyway. Right?
[i]Birthright: Gloat — every tantrum also brings down the Tower.[/i]

[b]Envy[/b]
Why would he cry his own tears when he could take yours? Envy can't shoot at all — but he flies, and every hit he takes feeds his jealousy. Take enough damage and he manifests orbital familiars: close-range guards, wide sweepers, and erratic zig-zagging wreckers, all upgrading through tiers and dealing contact damage that scales to absurd multipliers. The more the world hurts him, the more he takes from it.
[i]Birthright: Wish.[/i]

[b]Gluttony[/b]
A mountain of a boy with an appetite to match. Gluttony starts with 4 heart containers and swaps tears for a devastating Brimstone beam — but he's far too big to multitask. Moving interrupts his shot: plant your feet, charge, and erase whatever's in front of you. Flight? He'd love to see something lift him.
[i]Birthright: Gobble.[/i]

[b]Lust[/b]
Why fight when you can seduce? Lust's own tears are feeble, but she drifts through the basement wrapped in a charm aura that turns enemies against each other — and anything that dies while charmed gets back up as a permanent friend that follows her from room to room. Build the harem. Let it do the killing.
[i]Birthright: Desire.[/i]

[b]Wrath[/b]
Tears are for babies. Wrath doesn't shoot — he throws bombs, and only bombs, starting with 10 and an obscene explosion damage stat. Enemies drop bombs when they die (grab them fast, they don't stick around), bosses drop handfuls, and taking a hit sets HIM off — literally. Reach the Depths and he unlocks Deep Pockets: a bomb bank that stockpiles up to 999. Run dry, and you're just an angry kid in a basement.
[i]Birthright: Destroy.[/i]

[h2]The Tainted Sins[/h2]

[b]Tainted Sloth[/b]
Effort is a scam. His draw is even slower — but he constantly dribbles toxic creep behind him, and standing in his own filth doubles his fire rate. Fights from the puddle like nature intended. Lives exclusively on rotten hearts (red hearts rot on contact, soul hearts refuse to stick), and blue flies born near him grow up into loyal charmed Chargers.

[b]Tainted Pride[/b]
The scream, sharpened. Keeps the stat edge and the Reeee!, plus a random card to start. Still deciding exactly how insufferable to make him — actively in development.

[b]Tainted Envy[/b]
Jealousy with a body double. He can't shoot either — instead, your fire keys steer a clone around the room. Every orbital earned through pain alternates between you and your double, and every ten seconds (after a warning flash) you SWAP PLACES with it. Position both halves or die by your own hand. Runs on soul hearts only and starts with black hearts.

[b]Tainted Gluttony[/b]
Still marinating. In development — expect something big.

[b]Tainted Lust[/b]
Heartbroken, and running from it. Starts with a staggering 11 broken hearts and almost no damage, but blistering speed and a trail of blood creep wherever she goes. During boss fights, Empty Hearts appear around the arena — snatch them mid-fight to build Devil and Angel Deal chance, but take one hit and they shatter. Every boss you fell mends one broken heart. Outrun the heartbreak, one boss at a time.

[b]Tainted Wrath[/b]
A ticking time bomb. Literally. His health bar is a row of bomb hearts with a burning fuse — every 30 seconds the fuse burns down, a heart detonates, and a troll bomb hits the floor at his feet. Heart pickups do nothing; he patches himself up by grabbing bombs instead, and bosses drop five. Every hit rounds up to a full heart, and enemy explosions don't hurt him — they END him, Tower card and all. Sprint. The fuse doesn't wait.

[h2]State of the mod[/h2]
Apostasy is a work in progress and everything is subject to change. Most things work. Some things work in ways we don't fully understand. Balance feedback, bug reports, and ideas are very welcome — drop them in the comments and we'll keep them in mind.

Have fun, don't skill-issue.
